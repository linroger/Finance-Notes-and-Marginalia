# Lecture Notes - Financial Instruments

## Overview

This comprehensive collection covers fundamental and advanced topics in financial instruments, derivatives, and their pricing models. The notes are structured to provide both theoretical foundations and practical applications in modern financial markets.

## Course Structure

### Core Topics

1. **Derivatives Fundamentals**
   - Forward and futures contracts
   - Option pricing models
   - Risk management strategies

2. **Advanced Pricing Models**
   - Black-Scholes formula and its applications
   - Binomial tree models
   - Monte Carlo simulation methods

3. **Specialized Instruments**
   - Interest rate derivatives
   - Credit derivatives and default swaps
   - Exotic options and structured products

## Lecture and Teaching Notes Index

### Primary Lecture Notes

- **Lecture Note 5**: Black Scholes Formula - Detailed derivation and applications of the Black-Scholes option pricing model
- **Lecture Note 6**: Implied Volatility - Methods for extracting market expectations of volatility from option prices
- **Lecture Notes 9**: Corporate Securities and Credit Derivatives - Analysis of credit risk and derivative instruments
- **Lecture Notes 9A**: Credit Default Swaps - Comprehensive study of CDS contracts and pricing
- **Lecture Notes 10**: Interest Rate Derivatives - Fixed income instruments and interest rate modeling

### Teaching Notes

- **Teaching Note 1**: Forward Rates Agreement - Comprehensive guide to FRA contracts, forward rates, and currency markets
- **Teaching Note 2**: Futures Contracts - Futures markets, margining, and hedging strategies
- **Teaching Note 3**: Swaps - Interest rate swaps, currency swaps, and swap valuation
- **Teaching Note 4**: Multiperiod Binomial Trees - Multi-step binomial models for option pricing
- **Teaching Note 7**: Exotic Options and Derivative Pricing by Monte Carlo Simulation - Advanced simulation techniques
- **Teaching Note 8**: American Options - Early exercise features and pricing methodologies

## Key Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will:

1. Understand the fundamental principles of derivative instrument pricing
2. Apply mathematical models to real-world financial instruments
3. Analyze risk exposures and develop appropriate hedging strategies
4. Evaluate complex derivative structures and exotic options
5. Master computational methods for derivative valuation

## Prerequisites

- Solid foundation in probability theory and statistics
- Familiarity with financial markets and basic investment principles
- Calculus and linear algebra background
- Programming skills (recommended for computational assignments)
